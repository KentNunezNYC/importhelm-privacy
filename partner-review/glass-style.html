Liquid Glass vs flat tiles

The phone backgrounds are intentionally colorful so you can SEE the blur. On the right phone, the colored wallpaper bleeds through the icons. That's what makes it look like real glass. On a normal app screen the wallpaper is more subtle, but the principle is the same: light + color passes through, just softened.

What you're looking at

Left phone: the tiles are solid plastic-looking shapes. The colored wallpaper behind them is completely blocked. This is how the app looks today.

Right phone: the tiles let the wallpaper bleed through. Each tile has a soft colored tint plus a bright top reflection (like real glass under light). The bottom tab bar is also translucent.

Tap "Show modal preview" under each phone to see how a confirm dialog looks in each style. The right one matches the screenshot you sent.

Note: this only renders properly in modern browsers (Chrome, Edge, Safari, recent Firefox). If both phones look identical, your browser doesn't support backdrop-filter. The real iOS app will render it correctly via expo-blur on iOS 26.
